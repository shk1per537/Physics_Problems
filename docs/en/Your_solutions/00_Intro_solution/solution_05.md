## 5. Trigonometry

We are given a vector $\vec{A}$ with magnitude $|\vec{A}| = 15$ and an angle $\theta = 60^\circ$ with the horizontal axis. We need to find its horizontal ($A_x$) and vertical ($A_y$) components.

---

### Step 1: Calculate the horizontal component ($A_x$)

The horizontal component is adjacent to the angle, so we use the cosine function:

$$
A_x = |\vec{A}| \cos(\theta)
$$

Substituting the given values (knowing that $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$):

$$
A_x = 15 \cdot \cos(60^\circ) = 15 \cdot 0.5 = 7.5
$$

### Step 2: Calculate the vertical component ($A_y$)

The vertical component is opposite to the angle, so we use the sine function:

$$
A_y = |\vec{A}| \sin(\theta)
$$

Substituting the given values (knowing that $\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$):

$$
A_y = 15 \cdot \sin(60^\circ) = 15 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 12.99
$$

### Final Answer

The components of the vector $\vec{A}$ are:

$$
A_x = 7.5, \qquad A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 12.99
$$
