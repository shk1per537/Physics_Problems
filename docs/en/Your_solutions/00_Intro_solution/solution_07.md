## 7. Logic & Series

We are given:
* Initial distance to the wall: 10 m
* Speed of the bicycle ($v_{bike}$): 1 m/s
* Speed of the fly ($v_{fly}$): 2 m/s

Instead of calculating the complex infinite series of distances the fly covers by zigzagging back and forth, we can use a simpler logical approach based on time.

---

### Step 1: Calculate the total time

The fly keeps flying continuously until the bicycle reaches the wall. First, we need to find the total time ($t$) it takes for the bicycle to travel the 10 meters:

$$
t = \frac{d}{v_{bike}}
$$

$$
t = \frac{10}{1} = 10
$$

So, the bicycle is in motion for 10 seconds.

### Step 2: Calculate the fly's total distance

Since the fly is flying back and forth at a constant speed for this exact same 10-second duration, we can find its total distance ($D$) by simply multiplying its speed by this total time:

$$
D = v_{fly} \cdot t
$$

$$
D = 2 \cdot 10 = 20
$$

### Final Answer

The total distance the fly travels before being crushed is **20 meters**.
