## 4. Rearranging Formulas

We are given the formula for the period of a simple pendulum:

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

We need to rearrange this equation to solve for $g$ (acceleration due to gravity).

---

### Step 1: Isolate the square root

Divide both sides of the equation by $2\pi$:

$$
\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

### Step 2: Square both sides

To eliminate the square root, square both sides of the equation:

$$
\left( \frac{T}{2\pi} \right)^2 = \left( \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}} \right)^2
$$

$$
\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}
$$

### Step 3: Solve for $g$

First, multiply both sides by $g$ to get it out of the denominator:

$$
g \cdot \frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = L
$$

Now, multiply both sides by $4\pi^2$ and divide by $T^2$ to completely isolate $g$:

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}
$$

### Final Answer

The rearranged formula for the acceleration due to gravity $g$ is:

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}
$$
