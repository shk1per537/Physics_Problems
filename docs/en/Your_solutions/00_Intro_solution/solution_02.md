## 2. Systems of Equations

We need to find the values of $x$ and $y$ that satisfy the following system of equations:

$$
\begin{cases} 
2x + 3y = 12 \\ 
x - y = 1 
\end{cases}
$$

---

### Step 1: Express $x$ in terms of $y$

From the second equation ($x - y = 1$), we can express $x$:

$$
x = y + 1
$$

### Step 2: Substitute into the first equation

Substitute $x = y + 1$ into the first equation ($2x + 3y = 12$):

$$
2(y + 1) + 3y = 12
$$

### Step 3: Solve for $y$

Expand the brackets:

$$
2y + 2 + 3y = 12
$$

Combine the $y$ terms:

$$
5y + 2 = 12
$$

Subtract $2$ from both sides:

$$
5y = 10
$$

Divide by $5$:

$$
y = 2
$$

### Step 4: Find the value of $x$

Now, substitute the value of $y = 2$ back into the expression for $x$:

$$
x = 2 + 1
$$

$$
x = 3
$$

### Final Answer

The values that satisfy both equations are:

$$
x = 3, \qquad y = 2
$$
