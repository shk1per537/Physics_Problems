## 9. Momentum Comparison

We need to determine which object has a greater momentum:
* A fly: mass $m_f = 2$ g, velocity $v_f = 10$ m/s
* A tennis ball: mass $m_b = 60$ g, velocity $v_b = 1$ m/s

---

### Step 1: Define the momentum formula

The momentum ($p$) of an object is the product of its mass ($m$) and its velocity ($v$):

$$
p = m \cdot v
$$

### Step 2: Convert masses to standard SI units (kilograms)

For proper physics calculations, mass should be in kilograms ($1 \text{ kg} = 1000 \text{ g}$):

* Mass of the fly: $m_f = \frac{2}{1000} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$
* Mass of the tennis ball: $m_b = \frac{60}{1000} = 0.060 \text{ kg}$

### Step 3: Calculate the momentum of each object

**For the fly:**

$$
p_f = 0.002 \text{ kg} \cdot 10 \text{ m/s} = 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
$$

**For the tennis ball:**

$$
p_b = 0.060 \text{ kg} \cdot 1 \text{ m/s} = 0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
$$

### Step 4: Compare the values

Comparing the two momentums:

$$
0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s} > 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
$$

$$
p_b > p_f
$$

*(Note: Even without converting to kilograms, comparing $2 \cdot 10 = 20 \text{ g}\cdot\text{m/s}$ to $60 \cdot 1 = 60 \text{ g}\cdot\text{m/s}$ yields the same conclusion).*

### Final Answer

The **tennis ball** has greater momentum ($0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$) compared to the fly ($0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$).
