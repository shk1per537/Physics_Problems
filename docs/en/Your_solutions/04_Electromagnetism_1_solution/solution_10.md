## 10. Lorentz Force acting on Wire

We are calculating the magnetic force acting on a straight, current-carrying wire placed in a uniform magnetic field. This is often referred to as the Laplace force. The fundamental formula for the magnitude of this force ($F$) is:

$$
F = I \cdot L \cdot B \cdot \sin(\theta)
$$

Where:
* $I$ is the current flowing through the wire
* $L$ is the length of the wire inside the magnetic field
* $B$ is the strength of the uniform magnetic field
* $\theta$ is the angle between the direction of the current (the wire) and the magnetic field vectors.

*Note: The force is maximized when the wire is perpendicular to the field and drops to exactly zero when the wire runs parallel to the field lines.*

---

### Part A: Setting up the Base Calculation

**Given Constants:**
* Length of the wire ($L$) = 2.0 m
* Current ($I$) = 10 A
* Magnetic field ($B$) = 0.5 T

Before calculating for specific angles, let's find the maximum possible force ($F_{max}$), which represents the $I \cdot L \cdot B$ portion of our equation:

$$
F_{max} = 10 \cdot 2.0 \cdot 0.5
$$

$$
F_{max} = 10 \text{ N}
$$

Now we can easily find the force for any angle by multiplying this base value of 10 N by the sine of that specific angle.

### Part B: Calculating for Specific Angles

**a) Angle $\theta = 90^\circ$**
When the wire is entirely perpendicular to the magnetic field, $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$:

$$
F_{90^\circ} = 10 \cdot \sin(90^\circ)
$$

$$
F_{90^\circ} = 10 \cdot 1 = 10 \text{ N}
$$

**b) Angle $\theta = 45^\circ$**
When the wire is at a 45-degree angle to the magnetic field, $\sin(45^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707$:

$$
F_{45^\circ} = 10 \cdot \sin(45^\circ)
$$

$$
F_{45^\circ} \approx 10 \cdot 0.707 = 7.07 \text{ N}
$$

**c) Angle $\theta = 0^\circ$**
When the wire is completely parallel to the magnetic field, $\sin(0^\circ) = 0$:

$$
F_{0^\circ} = 10 \cdot \sin(0^\circ)
$$

$$
F_{0^\circ} = 10 \cdot 0 = 0 \text{ N}
$$

### Final Answer

The magnetic force acting on the wire depends entirely on its orientation relative to the field:
* a) At **90°**, the force is at its maximum: **10 N**.
* b) At **45°**, the force is approximately **7.07 N**.
* c) At **0°**, the force is exactly **0 N** (no magnetic force is exerted).
