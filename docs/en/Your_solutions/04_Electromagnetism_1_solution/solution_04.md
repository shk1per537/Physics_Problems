## 4. Force Comparison

We are comparing the fundamental forces acting between an electron and a proton within a hydrogen atom. The two forces at play are the electrostatic force, described by Coulomb's Law, and the gravitational force, described by Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation. 

The fundamental formulas for these forces are:

$$
F_e = k \cdot \frac{|q_1 \cdot q_2|}{r^2}
$$

$$
F_g = G \cdot \frac{m_1 \cdot m_2}{r^2}
$$

*Note: This comparison will demonstrate the massive difference in scale between electromagnetism and gravity at the subatomic level.*

---

### Part A: Electric Force ($F_e$)

**Given Constants:**
* Distance ($r$) = $5.3 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$
* Charge of a proton ($q_p$) = $+1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* Charge of an electron ($q_e$) = $-1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* Coulomb's constant ($k$) $\approx 8.99 \times 10^9 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$

Let's calculate the magnitude of the electric force. Since we only need the magnitude, we use the absolute values of the charges:

$$
F_e = (8.99 \times 10^9) \cdot \frac{|(1.60 \times 10^{-19}) \cdot (-1.60 \times 10^{-19})|}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}
$$

$$
F_e = (8.99 \times 10^9) \cdot \frac{2.56 \times 10^{-38}}{2.809 \times 10^{-21}}
$$

$$
F_e \approx 8.2 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N}
$$

### Part B: Gravitational Force ($F_g$)

**Given Constants:**
* Distance ($r$) = $5.3 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$ (remains the same)
* Mass of a proton ($m_p$) $\approx 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* Mass of an electron ($m_e$) $\approx 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
* Gravitational constant ($G$) $\approx 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$

Now, let's calculate the magnitude of the gravitational force:

$$
F_g = (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \cdot \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27}) \cdot (9.11 \times 10^{-31})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}
$$

$$
F_g = (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \cdot \frac{1.521 \times 10^{-57}}{2.809 \times 10^{-21}}
$$

$$
F_g \approx 3.6 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N}
$$

### Part C: The Ratio ($F_e / F_g$)

To find out how many times stronger the electric force is compared to the gravitational force, we divide $F_e$ by $F_g$:

$$
\text{Ratio} = \frac{F_e}{F_g} = \frac{8.2 \times 10^{-8}}{3.6 \times 10^{-47}}
$$

$$
\text{Ratio} \approx 2.3 \times 10^{39}
$$

### Final Answer

* The magnitude of the electric force is approximately **$8.2 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N}$**.
* The magnitude of the gravitational force is approximately **$3.6 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N}$**.
* The ratio of the electric force to the gravitational force ($F_e / F_g$) is approximately **$2.3 \times 10^{39}$**, which illustrates that the electric force is unimaginably stronger than gravity at the atomic scale.
