## 8. Lorentz Force

We are calculating the magnitude of the magnetic force (often called the Lorentz force) acting on a charged particle moving through a magnetic field. The fundamental formula for the magnitude of this force is:

$$
F = |q| \cdot v \cdot B \cdot \sin(\theta)
$$

Where $\theta$ is the angle between the velocity vector and the magnetic field vector. Since the particle enters *perpendicular* to the field, $\theta = 90^\circ$, and $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$. The formula simplifies to:

$$
F = |q| \cdot v \cdot B
$$

*Note: The mass of the particle ($m$) is provided in the problem description, but it is purely distractor information for this specific question. It is not needed to calculate the magnitude of the Lorentz force, though it would be necessary if we needed to find the particle's resulting acceleration or the radius of its circular path.*

---

### Part A: Calculating the Force

**Given Constants:**
* Charge ($q$) = $2 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* Mass ($m$) = $4 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$ (not required for this calculation)
* Magnetic field ($B$) = 0.5 T
* Velocity ($v$) = $10^6 \text{ m/s}$
* Angle ($\theta$) = $90^\circ$

Let's substitute the known, relevant values into our simplified formula:

$$
F = (2 \times 10^{-19}) \cdot (10^6) \cdot 0.5
$$

First, we can multiply the charge by the magnetic field ($2 \cdot 0.5 = 1$):

$$
F = (1 \times 10^{-19}) \cdot 10^6
$$

Now, we multiply by the velocity by adding the exponents ($-19 + 6 = -13$):

$$
F = 1 \times 10^{-13} \text{ N}
$$

### Final Answer

* The magnitude of the Lorentz force acting on the particle is **$10^{-13} \text{ N}$**.
