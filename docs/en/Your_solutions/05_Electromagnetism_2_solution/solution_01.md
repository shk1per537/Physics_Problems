## 1. Gauss's Law

We are calculating the total electric flux passing through a closed spherical surface due to an enclosed point charge. According to Gauss's Law, the net electric flux ($\Phi_E$) through any closed surface is equal to the net charge enclosed ($Q_{enc}$) divided by the vacuum permittivity ($\varepsilon_0$):

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{Q_{enc}}{\varepsilon_0}
$$

*Note: Notice that the radius of the spherical surface does not appear in Gauss's Law. The total electric flux depends solely on the amount of charge enclosed, regardless of the size or shape of the enclosing surface.*

---

### Part A: Calculating the Flux

**Given Constants:**
* Enclosed charge ($Q_{enc}$) = $+2\text{ C}$
* Radius of the sphere ($r$) = $1\text{ m}$ (not needed for total flux calculation)
* Vacuum permittivity ($\varepsilon_0$) $\approx 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \text{ C}^2/(\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2)$

Let's substitute the known values into Gauss's Law:

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{2}{8.854 \times 10^{-12}}
$$

$$
\Phi_E \approx 0.2259 \times 10^{12} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}
$$

Adjusting this to standard scientific notation:

$$
\Phi_E \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}
$$

### Final Answer

* The total electric flux through the spherical surface is approximately **$2.26 \times 10^{11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}$**.
* The $1\text{ m}$ radius is distractor information; the total flux would remain exactly the same for a sphere of any size, provided it completely encloses the $+2\text{ C}$ charge.
