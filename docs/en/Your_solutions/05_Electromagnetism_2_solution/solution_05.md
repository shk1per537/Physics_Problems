## 5. Energy Stored by charge in a capacitor

We are analyzing a parallel-plate capacitor to determine its fundamental electrical properties: capacitance, stored energy, electric field intensity, and the mechanical force of attraction between its plates.

*Note on notation: The problem description uses the letter $U$ to represent both the potential difference (voltage, $500 \text{ V}$) and the stored energy. To avoid mathematical confusion, we will use standard SI notation: $V$ for the voltage ($500 \text{ V}$) and $U$ for the stored electrical energy.*

---

### Part A: Capacitance ($C$)

**Given Constants:**
* Area of the plates ($S$) = $0.02 \text{ m}^2$
* Distance between plates ($d$) = $5 \text{ mm} = 0.005 \text{ m}$
* Vacuum permittivity ($\varepsilon_0$) $\approx 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \text{ F/m}$

The formula for the capacitance of a parallel-plate capacitor in a vacuum (or air) is:

$$
C = \frac{\varepsilon_0 \cdot S}{d}
$$

Let's substitute our values:

$$
C = \frac{(8.85 \times 10^{-12}) \cdot 0.02}{0.005}
$$

$$
C = (8.85 \times 10^{-12}) \cdot 4
$$

$$
C = 35.4 \times 10^{-12} \text{ F} = 35.4 \text{ pF}
$$

### Part B: Stored Energy ($U$)

**Given Constants:**
* Voltage ($V$) = $500 \text{ V}$
* Capacitance ($C$) = $35.4 \times 10^{-12} \text{ F}$

The energy stored in a charged capacitor is given by the formula:

$$
U = \frac{1}{2} C \cdot V^2
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
U = 0.5 \cdot (35.4 \times 10^{-12}) \cdot (500)^2
$$

$$
U = 17.7 \times 10^{-12} \cdot 250,000
$$

$$
U = 4.425 \times 10^{-6} \text{ J} = 4.425 \text{ }\mu\text{J}
$$

### Part C: Electric Field Intensity ($E$)

The electric field between two parallel plates is uniform and is calculated as the voltage divided by the distance between the plates:

$$
E = \frac{V}{d}
$$

$$
E = \frac{500}{0.005}
$$

$$
E = 100,000 \text{ V/m} \text{ (or N/C)}
$$

### Part D: Force of Attraction ($F$)

Because the plates carry opposite charges, they attract each other. The mechanical force of attraction can be elegantly derived from the stored energy ($U$) and the distance ($d$):

$$
F = \frac{U}{d}
$$

*Alternatively, this can be calculated as $F = \frac{1}{2} Q \cdot E$, yielding the exact same result.*

$$
F = \frac{4.425 \times 10^{-6}}{0.005}
$$

$$
F = 8.85 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N}
$$

### Final Answer

1. The capacitance ($C$) is **$35.4 \text{ pF}$**.
2. The stored energy ($U$) is **$4.425 \text{ }\mu\text{J}$**.
3. The electric field intensity ($E$) is **$10^5 \text{ V/m}$**.
4. The force of attraction ($F$) between the plates is **$8.85 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N}$**.
