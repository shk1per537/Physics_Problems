## 7. Wavelength and Frequency

We are analyzing a specific wavelength of light to determine its position in the visible spectrum and its corresponding frequency. The fundamental formula relating the speed of light ($c$), wavelength ($\lambda$), and frequency ($f$) is:

$$
c = \lambda \cdot f
$$

To find the frequency, we can rearrange this formula to solve for $f$:

$$
f = \frac{c}{\lambda}
$$

*Note: The speed of electromagnetic waves in a vacuum (including visible light) is a universal constant, approximately $3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$.*

---

### Part A: Identifying the Color

**Given:**
* Wavelength ($\lambda$) = 550 nm

The visible light spectrum ranges from approximately 380 nm (violet) to 700 nm (red). A wavelength of exactly 550 nm falls right in the middle of this spectrum. 

This specific wavelength corresponds to **yellow-green** light. It is no coincidence that the human eye is most sensitive here; evolutionary biology has optimized our vision to peak precisely at the most abundant wavelengths of sunlight reaching the Earth's surface.

### Part B: Calculating the Frequency

**Given Constants:**
* Wavelength ($\lambda$) = $550 \text{ nm} = 550 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$
* Speed of light ($c$) $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$

Let's substitute our known values into the rearranged formula:

$$
f = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{550 \times 10^{-9}}
$$

First, let's group the numbers and the powers of 10:

$$
f = \left(\frac{3.00}{550}\right) \cdot 10^{(8 - (-9))}
$$

$$
f \approx 0.005454 \cdot 10^{17} \text{ Hz}
$$

Now, we adjust this to proper scientific notation by moving the decimal point three places to the right:

$$
f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}
$$

### Final Answer

* A wavelength of 550 nm corresponds to the **yellow-green** color in the visible spectrum.
* The frequency of this light is approximately **$5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$** (which is also 545 THz).
