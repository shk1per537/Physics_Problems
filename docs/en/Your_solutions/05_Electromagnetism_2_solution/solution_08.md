## 8. EM Spectrum

We are asked to arrange various types of electromagnetic (EM) radiation in order of increasing wavelength. The electromagnetic spectrum encompasses all possible frequencies and wavelengths of electromagnetic radiation.

The fundamental relationship between wavelength ($\lambda$), frequency ($f$), and the speed of light ($c$) is:

$$
c = \lambda \cdot f
$$

Because the speed of light is constant in a vacuum, wavelength and frequency are **inversely proportional**. This means that radiation with the highest energy and frequency has the shortest wavelength, while radiation with the lowest energy and frequency has the longest wavelength.

---

### Part A: Analyzing the Spectrum

Let's evaluate the given types of radiation, starting from the most energetic (which corresponds to the shortest wavelength) and moving toward the least energetic (longest wavelength):

1. **Gamma rays:** These are produced by radioactive decay and other subatomic processes. They have the highest energy and frequency, meaning they have the **shortest** wavelengths (typically $< 10^{-11} \text{ m}$).
2. **X-rays:** Widely used in medical imaging, these have slightly lower energy than gamma rays, with wavelengths ranging from about $10^{-11} \text{ m}$ to $10^{-8} \text{ m}$.
3. **Ultraviolet (UV):** Responsible for sunburns, UV radiation falls just below the visible light spectrum in terms of wavelength (roughly $10^{-8} \text{ m}$ to $4 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$).
4. **Infrared (IR):** Often experienced as heat, infrared radiation has lower energy than visible light, with wavelengths longer than the red end of the visible spectrum (about $7 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$ to $10^{-3} \text{ m}$).
5. **Microwaves:** Used in radar and cooking, these have even lower energy, with wavelengths ranging from $1 \text{ mm}$ to $1 \text{ m}$.
6. **Radio waves:** Used for communication and broadcasting, these have the lowest frequency and energy, corresponding to the **longest** wavelengths in the entire EM spectrum (from $1 \text{ m}$ up to thousands of kilometers).

### Final Answer

In order of **increasing wavelength** (from shortest to longest), the correct sequence is:

1. **Gamma rays**
2. **X-rays**
3. **Ultraviolet**
4. **Infrared**
5. **Microwaves**
6. **Radio waves**
