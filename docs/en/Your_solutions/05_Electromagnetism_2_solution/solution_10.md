## 10. Speed of Light in Media

We are calculating the speed of light as it travels through a specific medium, in this case, a diamond. The fundamental formula relating the speed of light in a vacuum ($c$), the speed of light in the medium ($v$), and the medium's index of refraction ($n$) is:

$$
n = \frac{c}{v}
$$

To find the speed of light in the diamond, we rearrange this formula to solve for $v$:

$$
v = \frac{c}{n}
$$

*Note: The index of refraction is a dimensionless number that describes how much slower light travels in a material compared to a vacuum. Because diamond is extremely dense optically, it has one of the highest indices of refraction of any natural material, meaning light travels through it much slower than through air, water, or standard glass.*

---

### Part A: Calculating the Speed

**Given Constants:**
* Index of refraction of diamond ($n$) = 2.42
* Speed of light in a vacuum ($c$) $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$

Let's substitute our known values into the rearranged formula:

$$
v = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{2.42}
$$

First, let's divide the leading numbers:

$$
\frac{3.00}{2.42} \approx 1.23967
$$

Now, we reattach the power of 10:

$$
v \approx 1.23967 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

Rounding to three significant figures (matching the precision of the given index of refraction):

$$
v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

### Final Answer

* The speed of light in a diamond is approximately **$1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$** (or $124,000 \text{ km/s}$).
* This calculation shows that light travels at less than half of its maximum possible speed ($c$) when moving through a diamond!
