## 1. Wave Properties

We are analyzing the parameters of a sound wave as it travels through different mediums. The fundamental formula relating wave speed ($v$), wavelength ($\lambda$), and frequency ($f$) is:

$$
v = \lambda \cdot f
$$

To find the wavelength, we can rearrange this formula to solve for $\lambda$:

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

*Note: The frequency ($f$) of a sound wave is determined by the source and remains constant when the wave transitions from one medium to another.*

---

### Part A: Wavelength in Air

**Given:**
* Frequency ($f$) = 440 Hz
* Speed of sound in air ($v_{air}$) = 343 m/s

We need to find the wavelength in air ($\lambda_{air}$). Let's substitute the known values into our formula:

$$
\lambda_{air} = \frac{v_{air}}{f}
$$

$$
\lambda_{air} = \frac{343}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{air} \approx 0.780 \text{ m}
$$

### Part B: Wavelength in Water

**Given:**
* Frequency ($f$) = 440 Hz (remains the same)
* Speed of sound in water ($v_{water}$) = 1482 m/s

Now let's find the wavelength in water ($\lambda_{water}$). We use the same formula, substituting the new speed value:

$$
\lambda_{water} = \frac{v_{water}}{f}
$$

$$
\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{water} \approx 3.368 \text{ m}
$$

### Final Answer

* The wavelength of the sound wave in air is approximately **0.78 meters**.
* The wavelength of the same sound wave in water is significantly longer, at approximately **3.37 meters**.
