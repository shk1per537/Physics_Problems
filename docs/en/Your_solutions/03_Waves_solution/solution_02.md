## String Harmonics

We are analyzing the properties of a standing wave on a guitar string. For a string fixed at both ends, the fundamental frequency (first harmonic, which has exactly one antinode) corresponds to a wavelength that is twice the length of the string.

The formula relating the wavelength ($\lambda$) to the length of the string ($L$) for the fundamental frequency is:

$$
\lambda = 2L
$$

The fundamental formula relating wave speed ($v$), wavelength ($\lambda$), and frequency ($f$) is:

$$
v = \lambda \cdot f
$$

---

### Calculating Wave Speed

**Given:**
* Length of the string ($L$) = 64 cm = 0.64 m
* Fundamental frequency ($f$) = 330 Hz

First, we need to find the wavelength ($\lambda$) of the wave at the fundamental frequency:

$$
\lambda = 2 \cdot 0.64
$$

$$
\lambda = 1.28 \text{ m}
$$

Now, we can calculate the speed of the wave ($v$) by substituting the calculated wavelength and the given frequency into the wave speed equation:

$$
v = \lambda \cdot f
$$

$$
v = 1.28 \cdot 330
$$

$$
v = 422.4 \text{ m/s}
$$

### Final Answer

* The speed of the wave on the guitar string is **422.4 m/s**.
