## Wave Equation

We are analyzing the properties of a traveling wave given by its mathematical equation. The general form of a one-dimensional traveling wave equation is:

$$
y(x,t) = A\sin(kx - \omega t)
$$

where:
* $A$ is the amplitude
* $k$ is the wave number ($k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$)
* $\omega$ is the angular frequency ($\omega = 2\pi f$)

---

### Part A: Amplitude ($A$)

**Given Equation:** $y(x,t) = 0.05\sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)$

By comparing the given equation to the general form, the amplitude $A$ is the coefficient in front of the sine function.

$$
A = 0.05 \text{ m}
$$

### Part B: Wavelength ($\lambda$)

From the equation, the wave number $k$ is the coefficient of $x$.

$$
k = 2\pi \text{ rad/m}
$$

Using the relationship between wave number and wavelength ($k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$):

$$
2\pi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
$$

Solving for $\lambda$:

$$
\lambda = 1 \text{ m}
$$

### Part C: Frequency ($f$)

From the equation, the angular frequency $\omega$ is the coefficient of $t$.

$$
\omega = 50\pi \text{ rad/s}
$$

Using the relationship between angular frequency and standard frequency ($\omega = 2\pi f$):

$$
50\pi = 2\pi f
$$

Solving for $f$:

$$
f = 25 \text{ Hz}
$$

### Part D: Wave Speed ($v$)

The speed of the wave can be calculated using the relationship between wave speed, frequency, and wavelength ($v = \lambda \cdot f$), or by using the ratio of angular frequency to wave number ($v = \frac{\omega}{k}$). Let's use the calculated wavelength and frequency:

$$
v = \lambda \cdot f
$$

$$
v = 1 \cdot 25
$$

$$
v = 25 \text{ m/s}
$$

### Final Answer

* a) The amplitude $A$ is **0.05 meters**.
* b) The wavelength $\lambda$ is **1 meter**.
* c) The frequency $f$ is **25 Hz**.
* d) The wave speed $v$ is **25 m/s**.
