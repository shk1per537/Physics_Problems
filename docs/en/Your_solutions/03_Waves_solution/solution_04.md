## 4. Phase Difference

We are analyzing the relationship between the physical distance separating two points on a wave (path difference) and their corresponding difference in phase. 

The formula relating phase difference ($\Delta \phi$) in radians to path difference ($\Delta x$) is:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \cdot \Delta x
$$

where $\lambda$ is the wavelength of the wave.

---

### Calculating the Phase Difference

**Given:**
* Path difference ($\Delta x$) = $\frac{\lambda}{3}$

We need to find the phase difference ($\Delta \phi$). Substitute the given path difference into the formula:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \cdot \left( \frac{\lambda}{3} \right)
$$

The wavelength ($\lambda$) in the numerator and denominator cancels out:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3}
$$

### Final Answer

* The phase difference between two points on a wave separated by a distance of $\lambda/3$ is **$\frac{2\pi}{3}$ radians**.
