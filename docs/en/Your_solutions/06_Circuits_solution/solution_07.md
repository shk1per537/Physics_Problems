## 7. Capacitors in Parallel

We are calculating the total charge and total energy stored in a circuit with two capacitors connected in parallel. 

For capacitors in parallel, the total (or equivalent) capacitance is simply the sum of the individual capacitances. Additionally, in a parallel connection, the voltage across each capacitor is exactly the same as the total source voltage ($10\ \text{V}$).

---

### Part A: Equivalent Capacitance

**Given Constants:**
* Capacitor 1 ($C_1$) = $4\ \mu\text{F}$
* Capacitor 2 ($C_2$) = $6\ \mu\text{F}$
* Voltage ($V$) = $10\ \text{V}$

First, calculate the total equivalent capacitance ($C_{eq}$):

$$
C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2
$$

$$
C_{eq} = 4 + 6 = 10\ \mu\text{F}
$$

For use in our standard SI unit formulas, we convert this to Farads: $C_{eq} = 10 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{F}$.

### Part B: Total Charge Stored

The total charge ($Q_{total}$) stored by the equivalent capacitance is calculated using the formula:

$$
Q_{total} = C_{eq} \cdot V
$$

Substitute our known values:

$$
Q_{total} = (10 \times 10^{-6}) \cdot 10
$$

$$
Q_{total} = 100 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{C}
$$

This is commonly written as $100\ \mu\text{C}$.

### Part C: Total Energy Stored

The total electrical energy ($U_{total}$) stored in the capacitors can be calculated using the formula:

$$
U_{total} = \frac{1}{2} C_{eq} \cdot V^2
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
U_{total} = 0.5 \cdot (10 \times 10^{-6}) \cdot (10)^2
$$

$$
U_{total} = 0.5 \cdot (10 \times 10^{-6}) \cdot 100
$$

$$
U_{total} = 500 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{J}
$$

This is commonly written as $500\ \mu\text{J}$.

### Final Answer

* The total charge stored on the capacitors is **$100\ \mu\text{C}$** (or $10^{-4}\ \text{C}$).
* The total energy stored is **$500\ \mu\text{J}$** (or $5 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{J}$).
