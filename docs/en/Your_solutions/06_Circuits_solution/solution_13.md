## 13. Transformer Ratio

We need to determine the number of turns required in the secondary coil to achieve a specific step-down voltage. In an ideal transformer, the ratio of the voltages is directly proportional to the ratio of the number of turns in the coils.

The fundamental equation is:

$$
\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

---

### Part A: Calculating Secondary Turns

**Given Constants:**
* Primary voltage ($V_p$) = 120 V
* Secondary voltage ($V_s$) = 9.0 V
* Primary turns ($N_p$) = 400

First, let's rearrange the transformer equation to solve for the number of secondary turns ($N_s$):

$$
N_s = N_p \cdot \frac{V_s}{V_p}
$$

Now, substitute our known values into the equation:

$$
N_s = 400 \cdot \frac{9.0}{120}
$$

First, simplify the voltage ratio:

$$
\frac{9.0}{120} = 0.075
$$

Now, multiply by the number of primary turns:

$$
N_s = 400 \cdot 0.075
$$

$$
N_s = 30
$$

### Final Answer

* The secondary coil must have exactly **30 turns** to step the voltage down from 120 V to 9.0 V.
