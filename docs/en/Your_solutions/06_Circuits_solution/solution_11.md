## 11. Power & Energy

We are determining two fundamental electrical quantities for a resistive circuit: the rate at which electrical energy is converted into heat (power), and the total amount of energy consumed over a specific period.

The primary formulas we will use are:
* **Power ($P$):** $P = \frac{V^2}{R}$
* **Energy ($E$):** $E = P \cdot t$

*Note: To calculate energy in standard SI units (Joules), time must be measured in seconds, not minutes.*

---

### Part A: Calculating Power

**Given Constants:**
* Voltage ($V$) = $50\ \text{V}$
* Resistance ($R$) = $100\ \Omega$

Using the power formula relating voltage and resistance:

$$
P = \frac{V^2}{R}
$$

$$
P = \frac{50^2}{100}
$$

$$
P = \frac{2500}{100}
$$

$$
P = 25\ \text{W}
$$

### Part B: Calculating Energy

**Given Constants:**
* Power ($P$) = $25\ \text{W}$ (calculated in Part A)
* Time ($t$) = $5\ \text{minutes}$

First, convert the time from minutes to seconds:

$$
t = 5 \cdot 60 = 300\ \text{s}
$$

Now, multiply the power by the time to find the total energy consumed:

$$
E = 25 \cdot 300
$$

$$
E = 7500\ \text{J}
$$

### Final Answer

* The power dissipated by the resistor is **$25\ \text{W}$**.
* The total energy consumed in 5 minutes is **$7500\ \text{J}$** (which can also be written as **$7.5\ \text{kJ}$**).
